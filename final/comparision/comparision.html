Software Comparison

Photoshop, CSP, SAI, and Traditional Painting

Why I Made This Comparison

Artists can create work in many different ways, and each tool has its own strengths. Some are better for editing, some are better for line art, and some give a more direct physical painting experience. This page compares several common choices that artists use.

I chose Photoshop, Clip Studio Paint, PaintTool SAI, and traditional painting because they show different workflows for illustration and art making.

Comparison Table

Tool / Method Best For Main Strengths Limits Reference
Adobe Photoshop Painting, photo editing, mixed media Strong image editing tools, layer-based workflow, and timeline animation support. It is useful for artists who also need editing and compositing. Can feel more general-purpose than drawing-focused software, and the subscription cost may be harder for some students. Adobe Photoshop Features
Adobe Animation Overview
Clip Studio Paint Manga, illustration, comics, animation Built for drawing, with brush tools, vector layers, 3D reference models, and animation features. It is very strong for line art and character work. Less focused on heavy photo editing compared with Photoshop. Clip Studio Paint Features
Clip Studio Function List
PaintTool SAI Simple digital painting, clean line art Lightweight, stable, tablet-friendly, and known for smooth anti-aliased brush lines. It is often easier for beginners who want a simple interface. Fewer advanced features and less all-in-one support than Photoshop or CSP. PaintTool SAI Official Page
Traditional Painting Physical art practice, texture, material-based work Real paint gives physical texture, direct brush control, and a hands-on process that is different from screen-based work. Materials cost money, mistakes are harder to undo, and editing is not as flexible as digital work. Britannica: Painting

My View

If the goal is illustration and line art, Clip Studio Paint is one of the strongest choices. Photoshop is more flexible when editing and combining images. SAI is simpler and easier to focus on for pure drawing. Traditional painting is different from all of them because it gives a real surface, real materials, and a more physical process.
